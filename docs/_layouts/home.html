About

ecologies is an interactive environment for designing 2d sound ecologies with monome grid and Ableton Live. It works like this:

  • Arrange cells on a 2d field.
  • Change a cell's structure to define behavior.
  • Change a cell's route to create channels.
  • Signals emerge from giver cells.
  • Signals play a cells note when they collide.

It is an M4L midi instrument that pairs especially well with drum racks and zoned instrument racks.

Inspired by tools like meadowphysics, Orca, and Sunvox. But also other types of software like Dwarf Fortress, the tower defense genre, and the Eno / Chilvers suite of generative applications.

Also carbon-based life, water, the anthropocene, supply chains, The Mushroom at the End of the World, acrologies, The Dark Mountain Project, Eco Futurism Corp, "seasteading", the gedankenexperiment-prompt: "sound artist's work in a William Gibson novel?", Conway's Game of Life, and Garfield Park Conservatory.

ecologies is free, open source, licensed under a CC By 4.0 License.

grids 64, 128, & 256

ecologies was built for grid 128 but is lovingly compatible with 64 and 256. Just edit the .amxd with your grid's width and height. Then save and re-initialize.

This is a little buried because it isn't designed to be changed while playing.

Concepts

Term Definition
Field Your grid is now a field where cells live, channels wind, and signals propagate.
Menu For configuring individual cell attributes. Also deleting.
Cell Stationary points of light on the field.
Structure Each cell has one structure. There are three types: giver, mover, taker.
Giver Births signals.
Mover Passes signals along to others.
Taker Devours signals.
Route Each cell has one of 17 available routes.
Signal Travels on channels.
Channel Line segments that connect cells. Or rays that terminate at the field boundary.
Speed The rate at which givers birth signals. An integer in the range of 1-6.

Cells

Cells are the building blocks of ecologies. In addition to their x / y coordinates, cells have four attributes:

  1. Route
  2. Structure
  3. Note
  4. Speed

Route

Structure

Givers

...send signals into the field via their routes. Signals call out when they are born.

Movers

...are connectors of channels, directing the flow of signals. Signals call out when they are routed by movers. If a signals collides with a mover, and the mover has multiple routes, the signal will be split among all other routes.

Takers

...devour signals. They don't care much for routes (specifically, the route doesn't affect the behavior of a taker.) Signals cry out when they are taken.

Note

A note in the range of C3 - B5 (midi 48-83).

Speed

Consider an ecology in 4/4 time. This table illustrates the rates at which givers birth signals:

Speed Frequency Notation
1 (slowest) Every 6 beats Dotted whole note
2 Every 5 beats Whole note tied with a quarter note
3 Every 4 beats Whole note
4 Every 3 beats Dotted half note
5 Every other beat Half note
6 (fastest) One per beat Quarter note

Signals

Signals have a position (x & y) and a direction (n, e, s, or w).

If a signals's channel terminates in any cell without a connecting route, the signal disappears into silence. Forgotten. The same is true if it wanders off the field. The same is true if they collide with a giver. If two (or more) signals collide, both (or all) will be destroyed. If two (or more) signals meet at a mover, the mover only sees one of them. If a channel disappears from beneath a signal, the signal will continue along. Perhaps you can help it will find its way again?

Menu Detail

The four cell attributes (route, structure, note, speed) may be configured in the menu.

Cycle through routes as you would on the field. Select one of three structures (giver, mover, taker). Choose notes with the midi palette.

Finally, you may delete cells with the bottom row of the menu. There is no "confirm" action. Fortune favors the brave.
