About

ecologies is an interactive environment for designing 2d sound ecologies with monome grid and Ableton Live. It works like this:

  • Arrange cells on a 2d field.
  • Change a cell's structure to define behavior.
  • Change a cell's route to create channels.
  • Signals emerge from hive cells.
  • Signals play a cells note when they collide.

It is an M4L midi instrument that pairs especially well with drum racks and zoned instrument racks.

Inspired by tools like meadowphysics, Orca, and Sunvox. But also other curios like Dwarf Fortress, the tower defense genre, and the Eno / Chilvers suite of generative applications.

Also carbon-based life, water, the anthropocene, supply chains, The Mushroom at the End of the World, acrologies, The Dark Mountain Project, Eco Futurism Corp, "seasteading", the gedankenexperiment-prompt: "sound artist's work in a William Gibson novel?", Conway's Game of Life, and Garfield Park Conservatory.

ecologies is free, open source, licensed under a CC By 4.0 License.

Installation

Double click the M4L device to install it.

ecologies was built for grid 128 but is lovingly compatible with 64 and 256. Just select your edition and click reset.

Cells

Cells are the building blocks of ecologies. In addition to their x / y coordinates, cells have four attributes:

  1. Route
  2. Structure
  3. Note
  4. Speed

Route

Routes direct the flow of signals. Each active cell must have one of 17 available routes enabled. Routes can be configued via both the field and the menu. Routes always cycle in the same order before looping back to the beginning.

Note you can only view/edit the route of one cell at a time.

Route Order

  1. All
  2. North East
  3. South East
  4. South West
  5. North West
  6. North South
  7. East West
  8. North East South
  9. East South West
  10. South West North
  11. West North East
  12. North
  13. East
  14. South
  15. West
  16. Random
  17. Shell

Route Behavior

"All" and cardinal direction routes emit (in the case of hives) or route (in the case of ports and nomads) in the directions you would expect. Hives emit signals from all their open routes at a rate as defined by their speed. Ports and nomads route incoming signals to every other open route. So if a signal enters a "North East South" port from the east, it will split the signal into two and send them out of the north and south routes on the next beat.

Random structures emit or route signals at random.

Shell structures neither emit nor route signals. They're useful for stopping the flow of signals.

Structure

Each active cell has a structure. Structure is only configured in the menu. Newly activated cells default to hives.

Hives

...send signals into the field via their routes. Signals call out when they are born.

Ports

...are connectors of channels, directing the flow of signals. Signals call out when they are routed by ports. If a signal collides with a port, and the port has multiple routes, the signal will be split among all other routes.

Nomads

...are unimplemented in the beta. They will move along predictable paths.

Note

A note in the range of C3 - B5 (midi 48-83). This note will play when a signal collides with the cell, or (in the case of hives) the signals are birthed.

Speed

Speed is the rate at which hives birth signals. Speed has no impact on ports and nomads (but you can still configure it in case the port or nomad ever mutate into a hive - there are some interesting live performance implications here.)

Speed is synced with Live's playback.

Consider an ecology in 4/4 time. This table illustrates the rates at which hives birth signals:

Speed Frequency Notation
1 (slowest) Every 6 beats Dotted whole note
2 Every 5 beats Whole note tied with a quarter note
3 Every 4 beats Whole note
4 Every 3 beats Dotted half note
5 Every other beat Half note
6 (fastest) One per beat Quarter note

Signals

Signals have a position (x & y) and a direction (n, e, s, or w).

If a signals's channel terminates in any cell without a connecting route, the signal disappears into silence. Forgotten. The same is true if it wanders off the field. The same is true if they collide with a hive. If two (or more) signals collide, both (or all) will be destroyed. If two (or more) signals meet at a port, the port only sees one of them. If a channel disappears from beneath a signal, the signal will continue along. Perhaps you can help it will find its way again?

Channels

Channels are defined by cell routes. Signals flow through channels.

The Field

At the field's resting state, active cells (hives, ports, nomads) will be displayed with a single, brightly illuminated LED. Signals will be displayed as single, slightly dimmer LEDs, moving about. This is the only state where signal flow can be analyzed.

There are three types of user interaction available on the field:

  1. Single Press
  2. Double Press
  3. Long Press

Note that when a cell is selected, signal flow is hidden.

Single Press

  • Single press an inactive cell to activate it. Newly activated default to hives with all routes open.
  • The cell will become selected and its routes and channels will be displayed.
  • Single press the selected cell again to cycle through routes.
  • With the cell still selected, single press any inactive cell to create a new cell.

Double Press

  • With a cell selected, double press any inactive cell to move it to that cell.

Long Press

  • Long press any active cell to open the menu for that cell.
  • If a cell is selected, a long press on any empty cell will deselect it and display signal flow again.
  • Long pressing any inactive cell is reserved for future functionality.

The Menu

Long press any active cell to open the menu:

Pressing any dimly lit key will close the menu.

Menu Detail

The four cell attributes (route, structure, note, speed) may be configured in the menu.

Cycle through routes as you would on the field. Select one of three structures (hive, port, nomad). Choose notes with the midi palette.

Finally, you may delete cells with the bottom row of the menu. There is no "confirm" action. Fortune favors the brave.

Midi Palette

The midi palette contains the notes C3-B#5. Press any key to set the note.

Pressing any dimly lit key will close the midi palette.
